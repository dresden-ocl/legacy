What is the Dresden OCL Toolkit?

The Dresden OCL Toolkit is all about the Object Constraint Language (OCL). OCL is part of the well-known Unified Modelling Language (UML). It extends the UML's graphical notation with the possiblity of adding more formally defined textual constraints, pre and postconditions on method invocations and on class structures as a whole. Many aspects of a model that cannot be expressed adequately with the graphical notation alone find their representation in OCL constraints.

Now where does the Dresden OCL Toolset come into play? As its name indicates it is not some standalone solution. Instead it forms a collection of tools designed to support use and processing of OCL constraints in various ways.

Who did the work?

Work on the Toolkit has started as early as in 1999 when an OCL base library has first been implemented in Java. Since the first version of the OCL compiler, core of the Toolkit, has been completed alongside a Diploma Thesis continoous work on and around the Toolkit have lead to growing piece of software. Contributors recruit mainly from students and scientific staff of the Software Engineering Group at the Dresden Technical University where the project is also coordinated. Contribution is however not restricted to this group so feel free to get in touch.
